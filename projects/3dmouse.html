3D Mouse

This project was the result of a group effort from my senior design group. We were given the opportunity to present a poster at the University of Pittsburgh's Design Expo, where our group showed off our 3D Mouse prototype to peers and other faculty. This poster is shown above.

What Does Our 3D Mouse Do?

The 3D Mouse was designed, manufactured, and iterated by our group to create a computer peripheral capable of interfacing more intuitively with 3D Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software. The CAD software we decided to interact with is Autodesk's Fusion 360. The mouse gives the user the ability to manipulate the camera that controls what the user sees in the Fusion 360 environment. In orbit mode, the trackball (or billiard ball in this prototype), has a one-to-one mapping. This means that when you rotate the trackball, the camera rotates around the environment in a similar motion. This rotational movement works for rotation around all 3 cartesian axes. In zoom and pan mode, the trackball behaves similarly to a normal mouse, where the user can move left or right, or up or down when panning. Similar with zoom mode, a rotation up or down will zoom the camera view in and out. To change between modes, the user is given two buttons to rotate between the 3 modes, which is indicated via the LEDs.


Hardware

The 3D Mouse is wireless, utilizing a seperate receiver device that is plugged in to the user's computer to interact with the Fusion 360 API and our custom software. The receiver and 3D Mouse device utilize a proprietary protocol created by Espressif to communicate wirelessly between the two embedded wireless microcontrollers. The device is also battery powered, giving over 6 hours of useable time to interact with a 3D model. The case surrounding the trackball and most of the mechanical rollers and couplings were 3D printed to help lower cost of the overall project. Overall, our 3D Mouse was very intuitive and fun to use in my opinion. Thanks to the 14-bit magnetic encoders tracking the postion of the trackball, the 3D Mouse proved to be a viable option for interacting with a 3D environment.
