Dylan Butler

Transmitter Signal Processor

I was head of my robotics team in High School, where I decided to improve our robot's driving capabilities by modifying the signal mixing of our wireless transmitter. Unfortunately, the transmitter we used did not have very many signal mixing capabilities built-in. Specifically, our team wanted to configure our robot to drive in "Tank" mode. I developed a circuit to read in the potentiometer values, convert them to a high-precision digital signal, then mix the signals according to the "Tank" mode operation. This board wound up in an enclosure and worked successfully during many of our battle bot competitions.
